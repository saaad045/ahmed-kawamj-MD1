Expert Care for Minor Injuries

Quick, effective treatment for injuries that need medical attention but aren't life-threatening.

At Advanced Internal Medicine, we provide comprehensive care for a wide range of minor injuries. From sports-related sprains to household accidents, our experienced medical team is equipped to diagnose, treat, and help you recover from common injuries efficiently.

Unlike emergency rooms that prioritize life-threatening conditions, our minor injuries service focuses on providing prompt attention for injuries that require medical care but don't constitute emergencies. This means shorter wait times and specialized care tailored to your specific injury.

Types of Minor Injuries We Treat

Comprehensive care for common non-emergency injuries.

Sprains & Strains

Injuries to ligaments (sprains) and muscles or tendons (strains).

  • Ankle sprains
  • Wrist strains
  • Knee ligament injuries
  • Pulled muscles
  • Whiplash injuries

Cuts & Lacerations

Treatment for wounds that may require cleaning, sutures, or dressing.

  • Deep cuts requiring stitches
  • Abrasion care
  • Puncture wounds
  • Wound infection treatment
  • Dressing changes

Minor Burns

Care for first and second-degree burns that don't cover large areas.

  • Thermal burns
  • Sunburn treatment
  • Chemical burn care
  • Blister management
  • Burn dressing

Our Treatment Process

Step-by-step care for effective injury management and recovery.

1

Assessment & Diagnosis

Thorough evaluation of your injury including visual examination, palpation, and range-of-motion testing. We may use imaging when necessary to rule out fractures or serious damage.

2

Pain Management

Immediate relief through appropriate pain medications, topical treatments, or localized injections to ensure your comfort during treatment and recovery.

3

Wound Care & Treatment

Thorough cleaning, debridement if necessary, and appropriate closure methods including sutures, staples, or adhesive strips for cuts and lacerations.

4

Stabilization & Support

Application of splints, braces, or wraps to immobilize and protect the injured area, promoting proper healing and preventing further damage.

5

Recovery Planning

Personalized recovery instructions including activity modifications, follow-up care, and referral to physical therapy if needed for optimal rehabilitation.

First Aid Guidance

What to do before you reach our clinic for common minor injuries.

RICE Method

Initial care for sprains, strains, and bruises.

  • Rest the injured area
  • Ice applied for 15-20 minutes
  • Compression with an elastic bandage
  • Elevation above heart level

Bleeding Control

Managing cuts and lacerations before medical care.

  • Apply direct pressure with clean cloth
  • Elevate the injured area
  • Clean with mild soap and water if possible
  • Cover with sterile dressing

Burn Care

Immediate response to minor burns.

  • Cool running water for 10-15 minutes
  • Do not apply ice directly
  • Cover with sterile, non-adhesive dressing
  • Avoid breaking blisters

When to Visit for Minor Injuries

Signs that your injury requires professional medical attention.

While some minor injuries can be treated at home, certain signs indicate the need for professional medical care. Visit our clinic if you experience any of the following:

  • Unable to Bear Weight

    If you can't put weight on an injured leg or use an injured arm

  • Deep or Large Wounds

    Cuts that are deep, gaping, or longer than 1/2 inch

  • Signs of Infection

    Increasing redness, swelling, warmth, or pus drainage

  • Numbness or Tingling

    Loss of sensation or pins-and-needles feeling in the injured area

Typical Recovery Timelines

What to expect during the healing process for common minor injuries.

Minor Cuts & Abrasions

3-10 days

Superficial wounds typically heal within a week with proper care. Deeper lacerations may take up to 2 weeks.

Mild Sprains

1-3 weeks

Grade 1 sprains usually heal within 1-3 weeks with rest, ice, compression, and elevation.

First-Degree Burns

3-6 days

Superficial burns typically heal within a week. Second-degree burns may take 2-3 weeks.

Preventing Minor Injuries

Proactive measures to reduce your risk of common injuries.

Many minor injuries can be prevented with proper precautions and safety awareness. Implementing these strategies can significantly reduce your risk of common accidents.

  • Proper Footwear

    Wear appropriate, well-fitting shoes for different activities to prevent slips, trips, and falls.

  • Home Safety

    Keep walkways clear, use non-slip mats, and ensure adequate lighting to prevent household accidents.

  • Proper Technique

    Use correct form during exercise and lifting to avoid strains and sprains.

  • Safety Equipment

    Use appropriate protective gear for sports, DIY projects, and work-related tasks.

Common Illnesses FAQs

Learn about frequent everyday illnesses, their symptoms, treatments, and prevention tips to help you stay healthy.

Some of the most common illnesses include the common cold, influenza (flu), fever, stomach infections, diarrhea, and sore throat. These usually resolve with rest and basic care.

A cold often causes sneezing, a runny nose, and mild fatigue. The flu comes on suddenly with high fever, body aches, severe fatigue, and sometimes chills.

You should see a doctor if you have a high fever lasting more than 3 days, difficulty breathing, chest pain, severe dehydration, or symptoms that worsen instead of improving.

Prevention includes washing hands regularly, staying hydrated, getting enough sleep, maintaining a healthy diet, avoiding contact with sick individuals, and staying up to date on vaccinations.
