Gender-Specific Healthcare

Understanding that women and men have unique health needs requiring specialized approaches.

At Advanced Internal Medicine, we recognize that biological differences between women and men result in distinct health risks, symptoms, and treatment responses. Our gender-specific health services are designed to address these differences with targeted, evidence-based care.

From reproductive health to hormone management and condition-specific screenings, our approach considers the whole person within the context of their gender-specific health needs. We provide compassionate, comprehensive care that respects your individual health journey.

Women’s & Men’s Health FAQs

Explore answers to common questions about women’s and men’s health — clear, simple, and designed to guide your wellness journey.

Women should schedule annual wellness exams, including screenings like Pap smears, breast exams, bone density checks, and reproductive health consultations based on age and risk factors.

Men benefit from regular physical exams, prostate health screenings, cholesterol and blood pressure checks, and age-specific cancer screenings for early detection.

Most adults should begin routine screenings in their 20s. However, specific tests such as mammograms for women and prostate exams for men are usually recommended starting in the 40s or 50s, depending on family history and risk.

A balanced diet, regular physical activity, adequate sleep, and stress management play a crucial role. Avoiding smoking and limiting alcohol also reduce risks for chronic illnesses in both men and women.
